The Challenge

A crisis of pace,
not intent

73% Decline in global wildlife since 1970
40% Of U.S. animal species at risk
1M Species facing extinction worldwide

Over decades, conservation organizations have protected millions of acres and built enduring institutions. But most were not designed to compete in a fast-moving, institutional marketplace. As undeveloped land becomes scarcer, competition for high-biodiversity properties has intensified — particularly from sophisticated real estate investors deploying flexible capital.

Traditional funding models remain process-driven and slow, introducing timing risk in a market defined by speed and sophistication. This growing imbalance between private-market capability and conservation capacity presents an urgent threat to biodiversity protection. Conservation must modernize its transactional model to secure the most critical landscapes.

Our Approach

Designed to compete — and win

Our mission is to protect one million scientifically prioritized acres by 2030 by strengthening conservation's ability to compete for the nation's most important biodiversity lands. WAF deploys capital and transactional expertise to initiate and close high-value conservation deals in 16 scientifically identified priority biodiversity regions across the continental U.S.

01

Investment Focus

WAF directs investment through continuous analysis of biodiversity data from leading scientific institutions, prioritizing properties with the highest biodiversity return on investment — enabling proactive pursuit of the most critical opportunities rather than reactive participation.

02

Expert Transaction Team

A senior team of specialists across conservation, real estate, finance, and complex negotiations. This team leads deal origination, structuring, and execution — elevating conservation transactions to private-market standards with tailored capital deployment.

03

Deal-Ready Capital

An actively managed fund enabling rapid deployment of early-stage capital. These investments establish momentum, improve negotiating position, and attract follow-on funding — with capital structured for recovery and recycling across multiple transactions.

04

Strategic Partnerships

WAF partners with established conservation organizations to close complex transactions, drawing on a national network spanning nonprofit, government, philanthropic, and private-sector stakeholders to assemble the support required for durable outcomes.

Where We Work

16 priority landscapes across America

WAF has identified 16 initial high-priority sites through a meta-analysis of leading biodiversity and conservation assessments — selected based on habitat connectivity, endemic biodiversity values, and enabling conditions like preexisting protected lands and availability of public funding.
