Catalyzing land protection at the speed and scale needed to preserve biodiversity

The Wild America Fund (WAF) is a conservation enterprise designed to address the rapid decline in U.S. biodiversity by accelerating the pace and scale of private land protection.
Our Work Summary

WAF strategically invests capital and expert talent into key acquisition projects to initiate, design, and close deals representing America's highest conservation-value properties in a select set of priority areas in the continental U.S. identified by leading scientists.

Wild America Fund

America has long led the world in protecting wild places. Now, that legacy is at risk. The next decade will determine the future of our most irreplaceable ecosystems, the biodiversity they hold, and the climate resilience they deliver.

Biodiversity’s future will be decided in the marketplace. We make conservation competitive

The Challenge

The Game has Changed

50% of U.S. wetlands lost
30% of U.S. bird populations declined
50% of U.S. grasslands at risk
70% of U.S. insect populations declined
40% of U.S. forest at risk of collapse
~40% of U.S. plants & animals risk extinction

Wildlife populations worldwide have declined by an average of 73% since 1970, and one million species face extinction. The United States—one of the world's ten most biodiverse countries—is not immune: 34% of plant species and 40% of animal species are at risk.

The land conservation game has changed. As undeveloped land becomes scarcer, competition for high-biodiversity properties has intensified, particularly from sophisticated real estate investors deploying flexible capital and specialized transactional expertise. Meanwhile, traditional funding models remain process-driven and slow, introducing timing risk and deal uncertainty in a market defined by speed, capital readiness, and sophistication.

Development will continue—housing, energy, and economic growth are unavoidable—but market-driven outcomes are determined by who can compete effectively at the transaction table. This growing imbalance between private-market capability and conservation capacity presents an urgent threat to biodiversity protection and climate resilience nationwide. Conservation must modernize its transactional model—deploying flexible capital and dedicated expertise—to secure the most scientifically critical landscapes at the urgency this moment demands.

Our Approach

The Wild America Model

Today's private sector is a formidable opponent.

Wild America Fund exists to level the playing field, ensuring biodiversity has a force equal to the competition it faces.

We bring senior transactional expertise to conservation, acting as a SWAT team alongside established organizations.

We partner with leading organizations to accelerate and strengthen land protection—originating priority deals, deploying capital strategically, and executing with precision when timing is critical.

Guided by a singular focus on biodiversity and climate resilience, we make conservation competitive.

01

Investment Focus

WAF directs investment through continuous analysis of biodiversity data from leading scientific institutions, prioritizing properties with the highest biodiversity return on investment — enabling proactive pursuit of the most critical opportunities rather than reactive participation.

02

Expert Transaction Team

A senior team of specialists across conservation, real estate, finance, and complex negotiations. This team leads deal origination, structuring, and execution — elevating conservation transactions to private-market standards with tailored capital deployment.

03

Deal-Ready Capital

An actively managed fund enabling rapid deployment of early-stage capital. These investments establish momentum, improve negotiating position, and attract follow-on funding — with capital structured for recovery and recycling across multiple transactions.

04

Strategic Partnerships

WAF partners with established conservation organizations to close complex transactions, drawing on a national network spanning nonprofit, government, philanthropic, and private-sector stakeholders to assemble the support required for durable outcomes.

Where We Work

Priority Biodiversity Landscapes Across the US

WAF has identified 16 high-priority sites for our initial engagement through a meta-analysis of leading biodiversity and conservation assessments — selected based on habitat connectivity, endemic biodiversity values, and enabling conditions like preexisting protected lands and availability of public funding.

The science-based refinement and selection of geographic priorities will be a continuous priority workstream for WAF. This effort is being advanced through expanded in-house conservation data and analytics capacity, and in partnership with leading scientific institutions, local conservation leaders, and GIS experts.

Initial Engagement

Gaviota Coast, Santa Barbara

The Gaviota Coast is a rugged 76-mile stretch of coastline and the largest remaining undeveloped coastal landscape in Southern California, as well as a biodiversity hotspot of global significance. Where the Pacific Ocean meets the mountains, this wild and connected landscape forms a rare Mediterranean ecosystem that supports remarkable biodiversity and serves as a refuge for iconic and imperiled species—including southern steelhead, white-tailed kites, endangered pollinators, and California’s state grass.

Co-founder | Advisor

Kim Elliman

Christopher (Kim) Elliman retired as CEO of the Open Space Institute in 2024, after 32 years with OSI. In those years, in over 1000 transactions, OSI conserved more than 2.5 million acres in the eastern United States and created or enhanced over 600 parks and protected lands. Mr. Elliman has recently cofounded (with Michael Mantell) the Wild America Fund, to accelerate the pace of biodiversity protection in the continental United States. Having developed the first map of nationally significant biodiversity, our goal is to protect 1 million acres of key habitat by 2030.

Prior to his conservation career, Mr. Elliman worked for investment management companies and diversified holding companies for 25 years. Specializing in retail food and land management companies, Elliman served as Chairman of Piggly Wiggly Southern and a founding Director of Fresh Fields Markets, later Whole Food Markets. He was President of Grey Siefert & Co, of Overhills Group, General Partner of Elmrock Partners, and Director of Barrett Associates, all financial services companies.

He has also served on corporate, non-profit and foundation boards, chairing The Geraldine R. Dodge Foundation; Overhills Foundation; Wild Wings Foundation; Samuel Freeman Charitable Trust; The Wilderness Society; Grow NYC; and as a trustee of the Wildlife Conservation Society; Black Rock Forest Preserve; Storm King Art Center; Center for Humans and Nature; the Dalton School, and on numerous advisory boards and investment committees. Mr. Elliman received his B.A. from Yale University and now serves on the Leadership Council Steering Committee of its School of the Environment.

Mr. Elliman was an award-winning journalist, acted in an Academy Award winning documentary, and worked in NYC government and in renewable energy early in his career. Mr. Elliman has received numerous awards for his work in conservation from Black Rock Forest, GrowNYC, Open Space Institute, Parks and Trails NY, the Adirondack Council, Palisades Park Conservancy, among others.

Mr. Elliman lives in NYC and has four grown children, a granddaughter, and is married to Jennifer Cunningham.

Co-founder | Advisor

Michael Mantell

Michael Mantell, Sacramento, California, is co-chair of the Wild America Fund, which he co-created with Kim Elliman to help preserve biodiversity in some of the most important regions of the continental United States. Prior to that, he founded and led the Resources Legacy Fund and its affiliated entities, the Fund for a Better Future and Shared Ascent Fund to design and implement initiatives for philanthropic foundations and individuals that result in a just and resilient world.

Since the late 1990s, he has designed and participated in programs and projects that broadened leadership and constituency for natural resources protection and climate change, while achieving extensive outcomes for land, park, freshwater, and ocean resources. Michael helped lead the 2010 campaign that prevented a rollback of California's landmark climate change legislation. From 2000–2018, he also chaired and participated in campaigns resulting in voters approving over $30 billion of investments in water, land, park, and ocean conservation.

Previously, as Undersecretary for Resources for the State of California, he oversaw the $2.8 billion budget of the Natural Resources Agency. Prior to that, he was General Counsel for the World Wildlife Fund and a Deputy City Attorney in Los Angeles.

He is author and co-author of several books and articles, the recipient of several national conservation awards, and serves on the boards of the Monterey Bay Aquarium and The Wilderness Society.
